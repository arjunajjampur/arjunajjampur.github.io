WILSON

Overview

I was part of Wilson's Driver Vs Driver reality TV show as an Industrial Design expert. It was a fantastic experience to be involved in a TV show, showcasing my entire design skillset from sketching, concept moldeing, visualization and branding.

The winner of this contest walked away with half a million dollars!

I walked away with a depth of expertise in golf clubs, a free driver and glowing skin - thanks to repeated applications of makeup.

My contribution

I helped design the runner up driver "Pivot". The concept itself was submitted by Gavin Wallin, a contestant but I was tasked with taking a napkin sketch to a hittable prototype.

This concept contained a unique interchangeable weight system, that was revolutionary.

Competition

The season finale was a head to head between top two drivers - Pivot and Triton.

The cool thing was that my design would lose to Triton, the winning driver designed by my manager Darren Chilton, a well-known Golf fanatic inside Altair.

Most of the details of this project is still under NDA but here are some comparisons between the top two drivers as we approached the season finale.

Challenges

Golf drivers are extremely complex with highly precise geometry, both on the inside and outside. The USGA handbook for equipment rules defines the constraints when designing drivers.

  1. For starters, every gram counts, the lighter you can make the head, the better.
  2. User experience was important when designing interchangeable weights.
  3. The surfaces had to be of high quality to facilitate smooth production, especially the fillets around edges and the transition from face to hosel.

Design

Drivers have lowest loft angle in a golf set, they can hit far but not high. The design of the face had to be so precise that often it involved moving one or two points! Here is a list of golf driver KPIs.

The driver is built by fusing 4 main parts - Crown, face, sole and hosel. Most drivers are made with titanium and towards the finale every 0.1g was important!

The show was held at Wilson HQ in the windy city of Chicago, and was one of the most interesting and challenging projects I have done so far.

Their incredible facility and supportive team gave me an opportunity to learn intricacies of golf club design.

Outcome

As someone who hits the tee once in a while, I now have renewed respect and admiration for what goes on inside that head everytime I swing my club!
