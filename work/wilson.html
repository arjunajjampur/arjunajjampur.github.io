WILSON

Overview

At Altair, we have been offering generative design to our customers since 2008.

Altair Inspire, launched in 2009 brought forward a completely new way to optimize 3D models. This was the first user experience focused Topology Optimzation software on the market. The philosophy was to place the power of generative design into the hands of anyone, persona-agnostic. Now designers could run optimizations and produce amazing results.

My contribution

I was tasked with creating a new way of capturing inputs for complex use cases. This involved both novel component design and brand new user-experience elements.

Problem

As Inspire grew in complexity, we found ourselves dealing with complicated inputs for several tools. Existing methods of capturing inputs were getting quite hairy.

The "Guide-bar" introduced with Inspire was sufficient to capture inputs for most tools but became increasingly hard to handle for newer tools that required multiple inputs. It started causing fatigue.

In some cases, the guidebar flowed across the entire width of the modeling window, forcing users to traverse this distance to complete a task.

Challenges

  1. Understand and map typical and edge case workflows
  2. Improve the workflow while adhering to user expectations
  3. Design a scalable method that is easy to comprehend, intuitive and can be applied across workflows within the same system

Discovery

I took an example of a tool called “extrude” and laid out the flow. I used the anchoring principle to carefully choose expected defaults. This came from both my own experience as a 3D modeler and data from informal interviews with other designers.

Next step was to perform some competitor analysis and I landed on a top left - bottom down approach to input capture. This changed the guidebar design to a guide panel one. I started exploring some options for information architecture.

Once required inputs are defined, additional options guide the user towards their final design. Here, I am looking at various sub-journeys that must be accommodated.

After talking with all stakeholders, we narrowed our design to a panel that satisfied the following criteria:

  1. Clear primary workflow selection using tabs
  2. Defined IA with group headers
  3. Novel “entity selector” component
  4. New methods for real time editing (toggle buttons instead of drop downs to save time)
  5. Mini guide bar to keep familiarity
  6. Natural progression from top left to bottom right

Outcome

The new guidepanel design was well received with over 20 teams adopting the design in first 6 months of delivery. During this redesign I also created a brand new entity selector component that made selecting multiple types of inputs in the same panel very easy.
