WILSON

Overview

I was part of Wilson's Driver Vs Driver reality TV show as an Industrial Designer. It was a fantastic experience showcasing my design skillset from visualization to branding.

The winner of this contest walked away with half a million dollars for submitting a winning driver design.

My contribution

I helped design the runner up driver "Pivot". The concept itself was submitted by Gavin Wallin, a contestant

This concept contained a unique interchangeable weight system, that was revolutionary.

Competition

The top two drivers were Pivot and Triton

Most of the details of this project is still under DNA but here are some comparisons between the top two drivers as we approached the season finale.

Challenges

Golf drivers are extremely complex engineering feats with highly precise geometry both on the inside and outside. The USGA handbook for equipment rules.

  1. For starters, every gram counts, the lighter you can make the head, the better
  2. User experience was important when designing interchangaable weights
  3. The surfaces had to be of high quality to facilitate smooth production

Design

The drivers have lowest loft angle in a golf set. The design of the face had to be so precise that often it involved moving one or two points! Here is a list of golf driver KPIs.

The driver is built by fusing 4 main parts - Crown, face, sole and hosel. Most drivers are made with titanium and towards the finale every 0.1g was important!

The show was held at Wilson HQ in Chicago and was one of the most interesting and challenging projects I have done so far.

Their incredible facility and supportive team gave me an opportunity to learn intricacies of golf club design.

Outcome

As someone who hits the tee once in a while, I now have renewed respect and admiration for what goes on inside that head everytime I swing my club!
